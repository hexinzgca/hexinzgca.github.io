    PhD. Thesis: Trajectory-Based Nonadiabatic Dynamics Methods from Quantum Phase Space Formulation

    Published in Peking University PhD Thesis, 2024

    Nonadiabatic dynamics plays a crucial role in electronic excitation processes across chemical, physical, biological, and materials sciences. This thesis proposes a novel constrained phase space mapping approach for multi-electronic state systems, aiming to uniformly address nonadiabatic problems involving both continuous degrees of freedom of nuclei and discrete degrees of freedom of electronic states. The approach leads to a unified phase space mapping framework and its corresponding trajectory-based approximations. Different from the traditional Meyer-Miller-Stock-Thoss harmonic oscillator-based mapping approach, the thesis constructs the exact mapping from the phase space formulation of quantum mechanics. The paper emphasizes the one-to-one correspondence/mapping between quantum operators and phase functions within nonadiabatic systems, including three key elements: 1) trajectory initial conditions sampled from appropriate constraint phase space; 2) phase space integral for time correlation function, where phase functions are included; and 3) equations of motion on phase space and their trajectory approximations.

    The thesis summarizes the concepts of mapping kernels and sampling manifolds in the constrained phase space of electronic states, and enumerates the diversity of definitions for sampling manifolds and phase functions. In particular, this study connects Cotton’s triangular window (TW) function to the constrained phase space through projection, proving that it is an exact phase function for two-state systems and satisfying the positive semi-definiteness for estimation of population. Furthermore, the study develops the weighted phase space (WPS), where the weighted mapping model (wMM) method provides a better description of the coherence/decoherence phenomena of electronic states. It is found that under cyclic focused sampling (CFS) condition, the weighted mapping model (wMM/CFS) connects the MM-Focused and GDTWA schemes, and corrects the population estimation in Ehrenfest dynamics through the limit of the weighted manifold, thereby examining the shortcomings of the Ehrenfest method. Within the theoretical framework of phase space representation, the thesis introduces the independent trajectory approximation picture, improves over the mean-field method by introducing commutator variables, and also proposes nonadiabatic field (NaF) trajectory approximations, which yield the consistent transition between Born-Oppenheimer single-state force characteristics and mean-field mixed force characteristics. These studies lay the foundation for applications in real systems.

    Furthermore, the study applies phase space nonadiabatic dynamics methods with the independent trajectory approximation, such as NaF-TW, to various practical chemical/biological systems, such as photoisomerization of double bonds, molecular motor simulation, photochemical reactions of small molecules, and nonadiabatic processes of rhodopsin, effectively verifying the potential of phase space methods in studying practical nonadiabatic problems. After describing a set of code frameworks “KIDS” for effectively solving nonadiabatic dynamics problems in phase space, the thesis further concludes and discusses some perspectives.
