    Research interests & orientation

    The AI4S (AI for Science) research primarily focuses on predicting the properties of functional materials and the targeted generation of their structures, encompassing both theoretical foundations and practical applications:

    • Fundamental Research: Theoretical aspects of chemical & physical problems; AI methods & AI4S framework.
    • Applied Interests: AI-driven scientific design; micro-/nano-scale electronics & chemistry; ML/DL for chemistry.
      1. Generative Model-Based AI4S: e.g. phase space (point/path) sampling on physical ensemble for understanding the thermodynamic and kinetic properties of materials.
      2. Dynamics Acceleration: Implementing techniques to accelerate molecular dynamics simulations, enhancing computational efficiency as well as accuracy.
      3. Quantum Distribution Corrections & Classical-to-Quantum Transition: Developing methods to correct and refine quantum distributions, ensuring accurate representation and simulation of quantum systems & bridging the gap between macroscopic and microscopic phenomena.
    • Exploratory Interests: Quantum biology; multiscale molecular simulation/modeling.

    Project experience

    Project (2024-2025): Served as a member of a startup team dedicated to wearable sensors and advanced flexible manufacturing. Focused on applying artificial intelligence (AI) skills for chemical problem-solving in material science. My work drives innovation in flexible sensors, health diagnostics, and industrial applications of novel materials. (Cooperating Mentor: Professor Yingying Zhang).

    Project (2023-2025): Designed and developed a nonadiabatic dynamics package PSiNad, integrating modular components, high-performance parallelization, and highly extensible Python interfaces. (Supervisor: Professor Jian Liu)

    Project (2022-2023): Contributed to the development of the main branch of OpenQMD, helping the team build an efficient ensemble molecular dynamics algorithm library based on forcefields. (Supervisor: Professor Jian Liu)

    Project (2019-2023): Developed a nonadiabatic mapping phase space theoretical framework and algorithms, including the CPS (Constraint Phase Space) framework and NaF (Nonadiabatic Field) algorithms, providing excellent descriptions of quantum coherence, environmental dissipation, and nuclear branching problems. Applications include nonadiabatic spectra, reaction rates, and material carrier mobility calculations. Published 9 SCI papers, including 5 as the first author and 1 as a co-first author. (Supervisor: Professor Jian Liu)

    Established new weighted phase space algorithms; Proposed new algorithms to reduce complexity based on perturbative response analysis; Applied the mapping theoretical framework to many-body problems, reducing direct product complexity to direct sum; Developed phase space methods combined with multi-configuration; Designed high-dimensional weighting functions using symmetry-invariant neural networks; Coupled virtual dynamics for fine-tuning non-adiabatic dynamics.

    Project (2023): Collaborated with the Zheng research group on high-precision electronic structure methods (CASPT2) to study fluorescence-induced aggregation luminescence. Developed on-the-fly first-principles non-adiabatic simulation methods. Published 1 SCI paper as a co-first author. (Supervisor: Professor Jian Liu & Professor Junrong Zheng)

    Project (2022): Conducted international collaboration with E. Pollak at the Weizmann Institute (via Zoom) on quantum non-adiabatic tunneling flight time studies. Developed absorbing potential boundary conditions to solve long-term evolution problems. Published 1 SCI paper as the first author. (Supervisor: Professor Jian Liu & Professor E. Pollak)
